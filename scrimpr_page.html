Yes, you can sign up to multiple platforms and claim their welcome bonuses. However, only do this if you’ll genuinely use the services. Consider:

  • Will you actually use all these accounts/services?
  • What are the ongoing fees or commitments?
  • Does it make sense to have multiple accounts in the same category?

Important Considerations Before Claiming

Tax Implications

Some bonuses may be subject to tax:

  • Cash bonuses: Bank switching and some other cash bonuses are typically tax-free as they’re considered incentives
  • Free shares (investing): The value of free shares when received may count as taxable income
  • Capital gains (investing): If shares increase in value before you sell, gains above the annual allowance (£3,000 for 2024/25) are taxable
  • ISA advantage (investing): Free shares placed in a Stocks & Shares ISA are protected from both taxes (though many platforms place bonuses in GIAs instead)

Ongoing Costs & Commitments

Free bonuses are attractive, but consider ongoing costs and commitments:

  • Investment platforms: Custody fees, trading fees, FX fees, and inactivity charges may apply
  • Bank accounts: Some have minimum balance requirements or monthly fees
  • Energy contracts: Check exit fees if you want to switch again
  • Subscription services: Ensure you cancel if you don’t want to continue after any trial period

For investment platforms, use my ISA platform fee calculator to compare total costs.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not reading full terms and conditions
  • Expecting maximum bonus values for investment offers (most get lower amounts)
  • Cancelling or withdrawing before hold periods end
  • Ignoring ongoing fees and commitments
  • Using incorrect referral links or codes
  • Forgetting about potential tax obligations
  • Opening accounts you won’t actually use
  • Missing eligibility requirements (e.g., must be new customer)

Frequently Asked Questions

Which UK sign-up offer gives the biggest bonus?

Bank switching offers typically provide the highest guaranteed bonuses at £100-£200 cash. For investment platforms, IG Trading offers up to £200 in free shares (though 60% receive £40-£50). Energy switching can provide £50-£75 credit.

Can I withdraw my bonus immediately?

It depends on the offer. Bank switching bonuses usually have no restrictions once paid. Investment platforms have hold periods (30 days to 12 months). Energy credits are applied to your bills. Always check the specific terms.

Do I pay tax on sign-up bonuses?

Most cash bonuses (bank switching, energy credits) are tax-free as they’re marketing incentives. Investment platform free shares may count as taxable income when received, and any gains when sold may be subject to capital gains tax above £3,000 (2024/25).

Can I use multiple sign-up offers?

Yes, you can claim multiple offers across different categories (banking, investing, energy) or even multiple offers within a category if you’ll genuinely use the services. However, consider ongoing fees, whether you’ll actively use all accounts, and if it makes practical sense.

What if my bonus doesn’t appear?

Check you’ve met all terms (minimum deposit/spend, correct referral link, processing time). If everything is correct, contact the provider’s customer service. Most resolve missing bonuses within 5-10 business days.

Are sign-up offers safe and legitimate?

The platforms and companies listed are legitimate. Financial services providers are FCA-regulated where required. Sign-up offers are genuine marketing incentives. However, for investment platforms, remember your capital is at risk – the value of investments can go down as well as up.
